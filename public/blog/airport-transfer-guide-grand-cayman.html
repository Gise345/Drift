Complete Guide: Airport Transfers in Grand Cayman

DG

Travel Guide

Landing at Owen Roberts International Airport (GCM) and wondering how to get to your hotel? You're not alone. It's one of the most common questions visitors ask when planning their trip to the Cayman Islands. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about airport transfers in Grand Cayman.

About Owen Roberts International Airport

Owen Roberts International Airport is Grand Cayman's only commercial airport, located just east of George Town, the island's capital. Despite being a small island, the airport handles millions of passengers annually, with direct flights from major cities across North America, the Caribbean, and beyond.

The airport is modern and well-organized, but compact. After clearing customs and immigration, you'll find yourself in the arrivals hall with easy access to ground transportation options.

2. Traditional Taxis

Taxis are available outside the arrivals terminal. While convenient, there are some things to consider:

  • Fares are government-regulated but can be higher than app-based alternatives
  • Cash is often preferred
  • Prices may vary based on number of passengers and luggage
  • Availability can be limited during peak arrival times

3. Hotel Shuttles

Some resorts and hotels offer airport shuttle services. Check with your accommodation before arrival. Keep in mind:

  • Usually need to be arranged in advance
  • May operate on fixed schedules
  • Costs vary by property

4. Rental Cars

If you plan to explore the island extensively, renting a car might make sense. Rental agencies are located at the airport. Remember:

  • Driving is on the LEFT side of the road in Cayman
  • You'll need a temporary Cayman driving permit
  • Parking can be limited in George Town and Seven Mile Beach

Popular Airport Routes and Approximate Distances

Here are the most common destinations from Owen Roberts International Airport:

Seven Mile Beach

Distance: 4-6 miles (depending on exact location)
Drive Time: 10-15 minutes
Notes: The most popular tourist destination. Home to major resorts including The Ritz-Carlton, Westin, and Marriott Beach Resort.

George Town

Distance: 2 miles
Drive Time: 5-10 minutes
Notes: The capital city and cruise port area. Great for shopping and dining.

West Bay

Distance: 8-10 miles
Drive Time: 15-20 minutes
Notes: Home to the Turtle Centre and Hell. Popular for day trips.

Bodden Town

Distance: 10 miles
Drive Time: 15-20 minutes
Notes: Historic district with local charm.

East End

Distance: 20-25 miles
Drive Time: 30-40 minutes
Notes: Quieter, more remote resorts. Beautiful coastline.

Rum Point

Distance: 25 miles
Drive Time: 35-45 minutes
Notes: North Side beach destination. Popular for day trips.

Tips for a Smooth Airport Transfer

Arriving During Peak Times

Grand Cayman sees the most arrivals between 1 PM and 6 PM when flights from major US cities land. During cruise ship days and high season (December-April), allow extra time for immigration and customs.

Currency

The Cayman Islands Dollar (KYD) is the local currency, but US dollars are widely accepted. With Drift, you pay through the app, so you don't need cash immediately upon arrival.

Connectivity

The airport has free WiFi, making it easy to book your Drift ride. Most international phone plans work in Cayman, but check with your carrier about roaming charges.

Luggage

Standard Drift rides can accommodate typical vacation luggage. If you're traveling with oversized items like surfboards or golf clubs, mention this in the app so your driver can be prepared.
