Top 10 Must-Visit Destinations in Grand Cayman

DG
Drift Global
Local Travel Experts
December 15, 2024
10 min read

Grand Cayman may be small — just 22 miles long and 8 miles wide — but it's packed with incredible destinations that draw visitors from around the world. Whether you're seeking pristine beaches, underwater adventures, or cultural experiences, this Caribbean gem has something for everyone.

As locals who've explored every corner of the island, we've put together this definitive guide to the top 10 places you absolutely must visit during your time in Grand Cayman.

1. Seven Mile Beach

Why Visit: Consistently ranked among the world's best beaches, Seven Mile Beach is Grand Cayman's crown jewel. The powdery white sand and crystal-clear turquoise waters create a picture-perfect Caribbean paradise.

What to Do:

  • Swimming and sunbathing on pristine sand
  • Water sports including paddleboarding and kayaking
  • Beachfront dining at world-class restaurants
  • Sunset watching — truly spectacular here
  • Snorkeling right off the beach

Getting There: Seven Mile Beach runs along the western coast, easily accessible from anywhere on the island. Use Drift for a quick, affordable ride from your accommodation.

2. Stingray City

Why Visit: One of the world's most unique wildlife experiences, Stingray City lets you wade in shallow water with dozens of friendly Southern Atlantic stingrays. It's a bucket-list experience you won't find anywhere else.

What to Do:

  • Feed and interact with wild stingrays
  • Snorkeling in crystal-clear waters
  • Photography opportunities unlike anywhere else
  • Learn about marine conservation

Getting There: Stingray City is located on a sandbar in the North Sound. Book a boat tour from one of the marinas. Use Drift to get to your tour departure point.

3. George Town

Why Visit: The capital of the Cayman Islands offers a blend of Caribbean charm, duty-free shopping, and rich history. It's the heart of island commerce and culture.

What to Do:

  • Duty-free shopping for jewelry, watches, and designer goods
  • Visit the Cayman Islands National Museum
  • Explore historic buildings and architecture
  • Waterfront dining with harbor views
  • Visit on cruise ship days for a vibrant atmosphere

Getting There: George Town is centrally located, just minutes from the airport. Drift makes getting downtown easy and affordable.

4. Rum Point

Why Visit: This laid-back beach on Grand Cayman's North Side offers a more secluded, authentic Caribbean experience away from the resort crowds.

What to Do:

  • Relax in a hammock under the casuarina trees
  • Enjoy the famous Mudslide cocktails at the beach bar
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding in calm waters
  • Barbecue lunch at the waterfront restaurant
  • Swimming in the gentle, shallow waters

Getting There: Rum Point is about 45 minutes from Seven Mile Beach. Book a Drift ride for a comfortable journey, or combine it with a Stingray City tour.

5. Cayman Turtle Centre

Why Visit: This marine wildlife encounter and conservation facility in West Bay lets you get up close with sea turtles, from hatchlings to full-grown adults.

What to Do:

  • Hold baby sea turtles
  • Swim in the Turtle Lagoon with larger turtles
  • Learn about conservation efforts
  • See sharks, birds, and other marine life
  • Splash Park for kids

Getting There: Located in West Bay, about 15-20 minutes from Seven Mile Beach. Use Drift for easy transportation.

6. Hell

Why Visit: Yes, it's a real place! This small area of jagged, black limestone formations looks otherworldly. It's quirky, unique, and makes for great photos and postcards.

What to Do:

  • View the dramatic rock formations
  • Send a postcard from Hell's post office
  • Meet "the Devil" at the gift shop
  • Learn about the geological history

Getting There: Hell is in West Bay, often combined with a visit to the Turtle Centre. Drift can take you to both in one trip.

7. Cayman Crystal Caves

Why Visit: These ancient cave systems, hidden beneath a tropical forest in North Side, feature stunning crystal formations, stalactites, and stalagmites formed over millions of years.

What to Do:

  • Guided tours through three cave systems
  • Learn about geological and cultural history
  • Nature walk through tropical forest
  • Photography of crystal formations

Getting There: Located in North Side, about 30 minutes from George Town. Drift makes the journey comfortable.

8. Smith Cove

Why Visit: This small public beach near George Town is a local favorite for its calm waters, natural rock formations, and excellent snorkeling.

What to Do:

  • Snorkeling among colorful fish
  • Sunset viewing from the rocky shore
  • Photography at the iconic gazebo
  • Picnicking under shady trees
  • Swimming in calm, protected waters

Getting There: Just south of George Town, Smith Cove is an easy Drift ride from anywhere on the West Side.

9. East End

Why Visit: The quieter, less developed eastern side of Grand Cayman offers rugged natural beauty, blowholes, and a glimpse of traditional Caymanian life.

What to Do:

  • Watch the famous blowholes spray during rough seas
  • Visit Queen Elizabeth II Botanic Park
  • Explore the historic Wreck of the Ten Sail site
  • Enjoy less crowded beaches
  • Experience local Caymanian culture

Getting There: East End is about 40 minutes from George Town. A Drift ride lets you relax and enjoy the scenic coastal drive.

10. Camana Bay

Why Visit: This master-planned waterfront town offers a modern Caribbean experience with excellent shopping, dining, and entertainment in a beautifully designed setting.

What to Do:

  • Shopping at boutiques and international brands
  • Dining at diverse restaurants
  • Climb the Observation Tower for panoramic views
  • Catch a movie at the cinema
  • Weekly farmers market and events

Getting There: Camana Bay is centrally located on Seven Mile Beach. Drift provides quick access from anywhere on the island.

Planning Your Grand Cayman Adventure

With so much to see and do, planning your time wisely is essential. Here are our recommendations:

If You Have 3 Days:

  • Day 1: Seven Mile Beach, Camana Bay, sunset dinner
  • Day 2: Stingray City tour, Rum Point afternoon
  • Day 3: George Town shopping, Turtle Centre, Hell

If You Have 5 Days:

Add Smith Cove snorkeling, East End exploration, and a visit to the Crystal Caves.

If You Have a Week:

Take your time! Revisit favorite spots, try different restaurants, and maybe even take a day trip to the Sister Islands (Cayman Brac and Little Cayman).
